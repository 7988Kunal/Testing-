Exam Syllabus

Gate 2027 DA & AI — CSE–AIML

Total Units
7
Total Marks
100
Unit-wise Topics
U1
Probability & Statistics
Foundation · ~14 Marks
▼
  • Counting, probability axioms, random variables
  • Moments, standard distributions (Binomial, Poisson, Normal)
  • Maximum likelihood estimation, Bayesian estimation
  • Conditional probability, Bayes theorem
  • Hypothesis testing, p-values, confidence intervals
U2
Linear Algebra
Core Mathematics · ~12 Marks
▼
  • Vector spaces, matrices, determinants
  • Eigenvalues & eigenvectors, SVD
  • PCA and dimensionality reduction
  • Norms, inner products, orthogonality
U3
Machine Learning
Core · ~22 Marks
▼
  • Supervised: Linear/Logistic Regression, SVM, Decision Trees, Random Forest
  • Unsupervised: k-Means, DBSCAN, Gaussian Mixture Models
  • Bias-variance tradeoff, overfitting, regularization (L1/L2)
  • Cross-validation, evaluation metrics (F1, ROC-AUC)
  • Ensemble methods: Bagging, Boosting, Gradient Boosting
U4
Deep Learning
Advanced · ~18 Marks
▼
  • Feedforward neural networks, activation functions, backpropagation
  • CNNs: convolution, pooling, architectures (AlexNet, ResNet, VGG)
  • RNNs, LSTMs, GRUs, sequence modeling
  • Transformers, self-attention, BERT/GPT overview
  • Optimizers: SGD, Adam, RMSProp; batch normalisation, dropout
U5
AI Search & Reasoning
Classical AI · ~10 Marks
▼
  • Uninformed search: BFS, DFS, IDS
  • Informed search: A*, greedy best-first, heuristics
  • Constraint Satisfaction Problems (CSP)
  • Propositional & first-order logic, resolution
  • Planning: STRIPS, PDDL basics
U6
Data Analysis & Engineering
DA Track · ~12 Marks
▼
  • Descriptive statistics, exploratory data analysis (EDA)
  • SQL: joins, aggregations, window functions
  • Data preprocessing: missing values, encoding, scaling
  • Feature engineering, feature selection
  • Time series basics: trend, seasonality, ARIMA
U7
General Aptitude
All sections · 15 Marks
▼
  • Verbal ability: reading comprehension, sentence correction
  • Numerical ability: arithmetic, percentages, ratios, speed-time
  • Logical reasoning: series, coding-decoding, syllogisms
📌 Reference Books: PRML (Bishop), Deep Learning (Goodfellow), Artificial Intelligence (Russell & Norvig), Pattern Classification (Duda), Hands-On ML (Géron).
